Scaling Last-Mile Banking: How a B2B2C Product Strategy Drove 40% Revenue Growth

Company Fino Payments Bank
Role Project & Product Manager
Duration Nov 2022 – Apr 2024
Team Size 25 Members
40%Annual Revenue Growth
40%Faster Turnaround
96%Compliance Rate
8+Products Launched

Context: Understanding the B2B2C Model

Fino Payments Bank operates a B2B2C model — providing digital banking infrastructure to 5–8 partner banks (B2B), who then deliver last-mile financial services to end customers through Fino's merchant network (B2C).

This creates a uniquely complex delivery environment: products must simultaneously satisfy partner bank technical requirements, RBI/BFSI compliance standards, and the lived experience of merchants serving customers in semi-urban and rural India. Getting any one layer wrong breaks the chain.

The Challenge

Partner banks needed robust, reliable digital financial products — AEPS, IMPS, TPCD, debit card transactions, ATM services, loan management, FASTag — delivered fast, compliant, and at scale. The existing product suite was fragmented, and the merchant device infrastructure (biometric POS) was aging, with L0-certified devices that no longer met UIDAI/RBI standards.

Turnaround times were slow, compliance tracking was manual, and go-to-market cycles stretched to months. Partner banks were beginning to look elsewhere.

My Role

Project & Product Manager — single point of contact (SPOC) between business, product, technology, and compliance teams across 5–8 partner banks. Leading a 25-member cross-functional team spanning product, engineering, QA, compliance, and field operations.

The Approach

Product Lifecycle Ownership

  • Owned end-to-end lifecycle for 8+ financial products: requirement gathering → solutioning → development → UAT → production rollout, with no gaps in accountability.
  • Embedded compliance as a design requirement — RBI, KYC, and AML guidelines shaped user story acceptance criteria from day one, not as a post-launch audit.
  • Introduced periodic KYC refresh flows and AML screening into onboarding journeys, achieving ~96% compliance across all product releases.
  • Managed device deployment, integration, and certification of Mantra, Morpho, and Aratek biometric POS solutions, enabling secure Aadhaar-based authentication.

Biometric POS Infrastructure Modernization

  • Led device upgrade initiative from legacy L0 to L1-certified biometric POS devices, coordinating with OEMs, tech teams, and partner banks across the network.
  • Managed device certification, field rollout logistics, and merchant training programs.
  • Improved authentication reliability and transaction success rates across banking channels — directly impacting merchant revenue and customer trust.
  • Worked closely with field operations teams to resolve device issues in real-time, ensuring uninterrupted merchant operations.

Team & Delivery Optimization

  • Directed a 25-member cross-functional team across product, engineering, QA, compliance, and field operations — managing across geographies and time zones.
  • Restructured delivery workflows using Agile/Kanban, reducing project turnaround time by 40% and enabling faster response to partner bank feature requests.
  • Established KPI tracking frameworks and presented performance metrics to senior leadership quarterly, ensuring full transparency on delivery health and financial outcomes.

"In a B2B2C model, the product is only as good as the merchant experience. Compliance is table stakes. What differentiates is speed — and speed comes from process discipline, not heroics."

Impact

  • Delivered AEPS, IMPS, TPCD, and 5+ additional digital financial products, contributing to a 40% increase in annual revenue for the partner banking portfolio.
  • Reduced project turnaround time by 40% through Agile process optimization and workflow restructuring.
  • Achieved ~96% regulatory compliance rate across all product launches — zero major audit findings across the entire portfolio.
  • L1 POS upgrade improved Aadhaar-based authentication reliability, reducing transaction failure rates and merchant complaints across the network.
  • Received the Consistent Exceptional Performer Award from Fino Payments Bank leadership.

Industry Implications

  • Last-mile banking in India is a multi-stakeholder delivery problem. PMs in this space need regulatory fluency, not just delivery velocity — the two must coexist.
  • Biometric POS modernization is a proxy for digital inclusion — infrastructure upgrades at the merchant level directly impact financial access for millions of unbanked Indians.
  • B2B2C product strategy requires managing two distinct customer experiences simultaneously — the partner bank and the end user. Optimizing for only one creates blind spots that erode the entire model.
